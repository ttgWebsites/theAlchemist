Chapter Summaries

  • Prologue
  • The novel opens to The Alchemist reading the story of Narcissus
  • Narcissus every day would spend hours staring deep into a lake because he was so enthralled with his reflection
  • One day while starring deeply at himself, Narcissus falls in the water
  • Unable to swim, Narcissus drowns
  • The goddess of the nearby forest asks the lake why its water had transformed into salty tears, believing the lake was crying for Narcissus
  • The lake however responds by stating that it was crying because it had enjoyed looking at its own reflection in Narcissus' eyes
  • Section 1
  • The narrator, which tells the story in the third person, tells of a shepherd by the name of Santiago who had brought his flock to an abandon church where he plans to spend the night
  • While sleeping Santiago has a disturbing dream that startles him awake
  • The reader does not learn of what the dream entails however
  • Santiago talks to his sheep and tells them of a girl he met the previous year on a business trip in which he was selling wool; the girl is the daughter of one of Santiago's customers
  • Santiago has a long conversation with the girl before the sale is made and begins developing feelings for her
  • After the sale in which Santiago sells the wool from four of his sheep, he is asked to return in a year to provide wool again
  • We also learn in this section that Santiago can read, a trait uncommon for shepherds
  • The story then leaps forward a year to four days before the shepherd is to return to the merchant and sell wool again
  • Santiago daydreams of the merchant's daughter and cannot wait to see her
  • While daydreaming he also thinks to himself a terrible thought, that he could kill each one of his sheep one by one and none would be the wiser until it was there turn
  • He is troubled by the way he thinks
  • That night while he sleeps he has the same startling dream he had a year earlier
  • In his dream Santiago has a flashback of when he was younger and told his father that he would rather travel than be a priest
  • His father was slightly disappointed because this job was not as respectable, however he provides Santiago with enough money to purchase a flock of sheep, and then encouraged Santiago to try the traveling life
  • Santiago wonders to himself if his sheep enjoy the life of traveling like he does, but he comes to realize that they only care about eating, kind of like his new infatuation with the merchant's daughter, as that is all that occupies his mind at the moment
  • Santiago remembers that a woman in a village nearby will interpret dreams so he heads to visit her
  • Section 2
  • Santiago meets the dream interpreter who begins to give him a palm reading, this however bothers Santiago as he fears gypsies and the woman is obviously a gypsy
  • He plans to leave, however the woman stops him by saying that she can help, and that even if he leaves he will be charged anyways
  • He decides to stay and explains his dream
  • During the dream Santiago is in a field with his flock when a little boy begins to play with the sheep
  • The boy grabs Santiago and they transport to the pyramids
  • The boy tells him they will find a treasure but before the location is revealed, Santiago wakes
  • The interpreter tells Santiago that she has decided not to charge him and that he just has to give her 10 percent of the treasure when he finds it
  • She says it has to exist because a child told him in a dream, and that he must go to the pyramids to find it
  • Santiago leaves and goes to buy a book in the village
  • While resting in the city and reading the book he has just purchased an Arab man approaches Santiago and begins to question him
  • The man tells Santiago that he has read the same book and that the book is a lie, no one can make their own fate, it is already chosen
  • Santiago disagrees and sites his choosing to become a shepherd in example
  • The man tells Santiago that he is the King of Salem and his name is Melchizedek, he also says that if Santiago gives him a tenth of his sheep, he will help him find the treasure
  • Santiago believes this is a scam set up by the dream interpreter, but the King provides the names of Santiago's parents, names Santiago had not mentioned to the interpreter
  • Section 3
  • The King tells Santiago of a concept called a Personal Legend; the Personal Legend is something a person dreams of achieving as a child but eventually gives up on
  • The King then asks Santiago why he decided on the role of a shepherd, Santiago explains that he liked to travel
  • And the King points to the baker saying he also likes to travel but became a baker because it is more respectable
  • The King tells Santiago that he appeared because Santiago was about to give up on his Personal Legend, and that he comes to everyone about to give up
  • He is willing to offer assistance fore a tenth of his sheep
  • Santiago becomes upset with the King and travels on in the city, stopping to buy bread from the baker
  • He considers traveling to Africa on a boat, but instead decides to continue on to the merchant and his daughter, because he believes that this is the logical next step in his Personal Legend
  • The next day he comes across the king again and says he is willing to take up his proposal he adds that all he has left is six sheep as he sold the rest
  • The King tells him that the treasure is by the pyramids, which does not satisfy Santiago
  • A butterfly appears and the King says this is a good omen; the King then opens his breastplate and reveals a jeweled cape, giving two jewels to Santiago
  • He tells Santiago that the jewels represent yes and no, and that they will help him interpret omens
  • Before the King leaves Santiago he tells a story of the son of a shopkeeper
  • The son was sent to the wisest man in the world to learn how to be truly happy
  • The man told the boy that he must be able to admire his castle without spilling a spoonful of oil that he is balancing
  • The boy first does not even notice the castle as he keeps watch on the oil, the second attempt the opposite occurs
  • The moral is that you must be able to balance your priorities to be happy
  • The King watches Santiago leave and thinks back to the same bargain he made with Abraham
  • Section 4
  • Santiago takes the boat to Tangier, in Africa, and when he arrives he goes to a bar
  • He sees people sharing pipes and holding hands, and he scorns the way they are acting
  • Santiago meets a young man in the bar that questions Santiago's intentions
  • Santiago explains he needs to get to the pyramids, which are all the way across the Sahara Desert
  • The man takes Santiago outside, and Santiago provides him with enough money for camels
  • The two men go to a marketplace to purchase provisions but the man gets separated from Santiago and does not return, Santiago is left alone realizing that he has been robbed
  • Santiago is very upset, and decides to inventory his remaining possessions
  • He still has his book, jacket, and stones, he considers selling the stones and returning home, but decides against it
  • Santiago eventually falls asleep at the marketplace and is woken up the next day when they begin to set up shop
  • Santiago sees a dirty crystal shop and decides to clean the windows in hopes of receiving food
  • The owner feeds Santiago after he is done working but says he did not need to clean the windows, as he would have fed him anyways
  • The man offers Santiago a job to clean because when Santiago was cleaning earlier two customers entered the shop
  • Santiago learns that the job will not pay for his trip to Egypt though
  • Section 5
  • Santiago works for about a month but is dissatisfied as it will still take over a year until he gets paid
  • Santiago wants to improve the store and increase sales but the crystal merchant doesn't seem to agree with him
  • Santiago had planned to build a display case thinking it would bring in more profits and hasten the rate it takes to make enough money for the trip to Egypt
  • At first, the merchant says that he does not want to do this, however after learning about Santiago's dream, and relating it to his own dream of traveling to Mecca he decides to go forth with the display case
  • Santiago also comes up with a new idea, to sell tea, and this further increases profits, eventually allowing for more employees to be hired
  • The shop makes a great deal of money, even making Santiago very rich, he plans to return home and buy another flock of sheep, and tells the merchant to travel to Mecca, but the merchant says that neither of those will occur as it is written that that is not their fates
  • While packing to leave, the two jewels he received from the King fall from his bag; Santiago remembers his dream to travel to Egypt and decides against returning home, he instead plans his trip to Egypt
  • Section 6
  • The novel introduces an Englishman who is preparing to take a trip through the Sahara desert as he plans to meet an alchemist who lives at the Al-Fayoum oasis, and learn from him
  • The alchemist apparently has discovered the Philosopher's Stone and Elixir of Life
  • Santiago joins this man's caravan through the Sahara in hopes of reaching Egypt
  • At first Santiago tries to tell the man of his story at the crystal shop and the story of the jewels he received from the King, however the Englishman is not impressed
  • The caravan heads off and the leader makes the men swear they will obey him
  • Santiago has a conversation with the camel driver, and he tells his story of being a shepherd, the driver tells how he ran an orchard, however one day it was ruined by a flood, and he had to find a new profession, through that experience he learned not to become attached to material possessions
  • The caravan heads into a dangerous area of thieves, and bandits
  • They are quiet and avoid any dangerous confrontations
  • Santiago has another conversation with the Englishman later on in the journey, Santiago tells his life story and the Englishman compares it to the principles of alchemy, called the Soul of the World
  • Santiago decides he should learn more about this and he reads some alchemy books provided by the Englishman
  • He learns of an Emerald Tablet in which the most important alchemy test is inscribed
  • Santiago also reads a book about a topic called the Master Work
  • The Master Work entails two metals that once purified all the way down leave nothing but the Soul of the World
  • There is two parts of the Master Work, the Elixir of Life that cures all ills, and the Philosophers Stone, which transforms anything into gold
  • The Englishman tries to converse with Santiago about alchemy but is disappointed as Santiago doesn't really understand it
  • The caravan eventually reaches the oasis safely
  • Section 7
  • The Alchemist, waiting in Al-Fayoum, watches as the caravan arrives, he is unaffected by the arrival as he has seen many people come and go, but the town is very excited
  • The Alchemist learned from omens that someone in the caravan shall become his apprentice, he wonders to himself how worthy he or she will be
  • Santiago is amazed by the new town; it is much larger than many Spanish cities
  • Many women and children come up to the caravan with much interest, and Santiago learns that because of the large amount of women and children that live there, the town is relatively safe
  • The men on the caravan must remain at the oasis until an ongoing war ends, so they will share tents with locals and other travelers
  • The morning after arriving Santiago begins to tell those in his tent his life story when he is interrupted by the Englishman and is asked to help him find the alchemist
  • The men search and search for the alchemist and have no luck
  • Santiago decides to ask one last person, a young woman, however when he gazes upon her he falls deeply in love
  • The women, Fatima, explains that the alchemist lives in the southern part of the town
  • The Englishman continues the search but Santiago stays put, struck by love
  • The next day he searches for Fatima again going to the well
  • At the well he sees the Englishman
  • The Englishman tells of finding the alchemist, but being disappointed by what occurred, as he did not teach him anything yet
  • After the Englishman leaves Santiago meets Fatima again, he confesses his deep love for her
  • The war drags on and the meeting at the well occurs everyday for a while, the two become closer and Santiago tells of his quest to find treasure, and his Personal Legend
  • Fatima tells Santiago that she has been waiting her whole life for him, but he must continue on his quest to Egypt, and that if they meet again they are destined to be together
  • Santiago goes and finds the Englishman again and learns that he has made a furnace, he plans to learn how to turn metals into gold, and he plans to continue on the Master Work as instructed by the alchemist
  • Section 8
  • Santiago has a vision of soldiers riding through the oasis
  • Remembering what he was told by the King, that he should take omens seriously, he tells the caravan driver who believes what Santiago says
  • The camel driver recalls an encounter with a seer, as they make there living by understanding the Soul of the World
  • The seer had wondered why the driver had wanted to know the future yet the driver did not have a good answer
  • The seer explained that we should not know or worry about the future and only sometimes it is revealed to us so that we might change it
  • The driver, recalling this story, tells Santiago to go tell the chieftains of Santiago's vision, as they should take it seriously
  • Santiago goes to tell the chieftains, and after speaking to a guard and a young Arab man dressed in white and gold, he is eventually invited in to speak to the chieftains
  • The chieftains debate in Arabic and eventually an older, wise chieftain, speaks up, telling the story of Joseph, a man who believed in the power of dreams, he was sold as a slave in Egypt and interpreted the Pharaoh's dreams and ultimately saved Egypt from famine
  • The old man says that because of this dreams should be taken seriously
  • The old man says he will release a weapon ban on the oasis and that every man will be rewarded for every ten men he kills
  • However, if Santiago is wrong, Santiago will be killed
  • Section 9
  • When Santiago leaves the chieftains he comes across a horseman in black who attacks him
  • The horseman then asks how dare Santiago defy Allah and interpret a dream
  • Santiago replies that he saw into the Soul of the World, and that Allah willed the vision to occur
  • The horseman settles down slightly and asks Santiago why he is in the desert and Santiago responds by telling of his Personal Legend
  • The man then tells Santiago he needs to test his courage, and to not give up his goal
  • Santiago is told to meet the man the next day after sunset if he survives the ensuing battle
  • Santiago learns that the man lives in the south; the reader also learns that the horseman is the alchemist
  • The next day the citizens of Al-Fayoum are on alert, and sure enough an army of 500 men approach ready to attack
  • A short battle occurs but all the villains are eventually defeated and killed except for the commander of the army
  • They question the commander and learn that he attacked because his men were starving and needed sustenance to continue the war
  • The commander is sentenced to death and Santiago is rewarded for his vision with 50 gold pieces and is asked to become a tribal captain
  • That night Santiago goes to find the alchemist
  • He enters his tent as the alchemist tells him that he has had omens that Santiago would need his help
  • The alchemist says he is supposed to help Santiago find his treasure
  • Santiago explains that he has his treasure, the gold coins, Fatima, and his camel
  • But the alchemist says he still needs to seek the treasure from Egypt, and that Santiago should sell his camel and buy a horse
  • Section 10
  • Santiago goes back to see the alchemist the next night with a horse
  • The alchemist tells Santiago that he needs to lead him to where there is life in the forest
  • Santiago is at first confused but then learns that life attracts life
  • Santiago and the alchemist gallop off into the desert until suddenly the horses slow down, Santiago remarks that there is life near, as his horse knows other life
  • Santiago finds a cobra in the sand and it hisses at him, the alchemist grabs the cobra, draws a circle in the sand, and places the cobra in it, immediately settling it down
  • The alchemist says the snake will not leave the circle
  • The alchemist says they should continue on to Egypt for the treasure, but Santiago does not want to leave Fatima
  • The alchemist says that even though that is understandable, it is not the best option
  • Initially Santiago would lead a wonderful life, he would be rich, and marry Fatima, however after a few years, he would be filled with regret, and his life would spiral out of control because of it
  • The men return to the oasis for one night to prepare their journey
  • Santiago sees Fatima and tells her he loves her and he will return
  • They begin the journey, and after about a week they set up camp early one night, and the alchemist tells Santiago that it is nearly over
  • Santiago is disappointed, he has not learned anything, but the alchemist explains that he should have through watching the alchemist's actions
  • The alchemist explains that he learned his trade from his grandfather back when alchemy was much simpler
  • He says books have complicated alchemy, and that back in the day all they needed was the Emerald Tablet to understand alchemy
  • Santiago asks what the tablet says but the alchemist says it is not easy to explain, and that in order to do so he must immerse himself in the desert and understand the Soul of the World
  • Section 11
  • Santiago and the Alchemist travel through the most dangerous area of the tribal fights
  • Even though Santiago doesn't want to continue, the Alchemist encourages him to follow on and follow his dream
  • After they awake the next morning, Santiago feels better and continues on
  • While traveling the next day, three tribesman search Santiago and the Alchemist
  • When they find The Alchemist's Philosopher's Stone and Elixir of Life, they don't believe that they have magical powers
  • When they are allowed to leave, the Alchemist explains to Santiago that people rarely believe you when you say you are carrying treasure
  • When Santiago is no longer scared of traveling, two men appear behind him and the Alchemist
  • The Alchemist uses the vision of his soul to let them keep traveling
  • While only two days from the pyramids, Santiago inquires about alchemy, specifically how to turn lead into gold
  • The Alchemist explains that gold is the most evolved metal and only the most successful alchemists understand how to do that procedure
  • Santiago and the Alchemist encounter Arabic tribesmen that accuse them of being spies
  • The Alchemist saves them by claiming that Santiago is an alchemist and that he will blow them away by controlling the wind
  • He then tells Santiago that only fear prevents someone from living out their Personal Legend
  • Section 12
  • While in their first day at the camp, Santiago worries that he will not be able to turn himself into the wind
  • The Alchemist assures him that he already knows how to
  • When the third day at the camp arrives, the officers and chiefs demand that Santiago turn into the wind
  • As he attempts to do this, he searches the desert and it offers sand to Santiago in order to help the wind blow
  • He also has to ask the wind for help
  • While struggling to make the wind pick up even more, he battles with it back and forth trying to convince it that he wants to be like the wind and reach all edges of the earth
  • In order to ask the “Hand That Wrote All” he asks for sand to be blown so he can look up into the sky
  • After asking the sun, it shines brighter causing the wind to blow harder to block out its brightness
  • After the sun rejects Santiago's request, he prays and asks for this miracle and realizes that he can perform his own miracles
  • The Alchemist and Santiago are released and they continue on their journey with an escort party provided by the chief
  • Section 13
  • When the group reaches the Coptic monastery, Santiago is told to finish the trip alone, he makes gold from lead and shares with the men
  • The Alchemist tells him a story of an emperor that had two sons, a soldier and a poet
  • After this, Santiago finishes his journey, when he arrives at the foot of the pyramids he finds nothing and is captured
  • The men think that since he has gold he found it in the sand and make him finish digging
  • When he finds nothing they beat him but he tells them that he knows where to find his treasure
  • Epilogue
  • Santiago is greatly amused when he realizes that God left him gold at the monastery but that God wanted him to see the pyramids and their beauty
  • He finds a chest of gold coins and jewels and decides to give a tenth to the gypsy
  • With his journey completed he can sense the kiss of Fatima's lips on his, he says he will be with her soon
